THE PROBLEM AND WHAT WE OFFER

The Hubble tension (4-6 sigma discrepancy between CMB-based H0 ~ 67
km/s/Mpc and SH0ES H0 ~ 73 km/s/Mpc) is one of the most debated
questions in modern cosmology. Proposed solutions range from early dark
energy to modified gravity, but none has achieved consensus. Most
analyses treat the cosmological constant Lambda as a free parameter;
no prior work has derived Lambda from first principles and used that
derivation as a theoretical prior.

This paper makes three contributions:

1. FIRST-PRINCIPLES DERIVATION. We derive Lambda from the Cohen-Kaplan-
   Nelson (1999) UV-IR holographic collapse bound with zero free
   parameters. The derivation is a theorem, not a fit: it follows from
   the holographic principle and the Friedmann equation alone. The exact
   identity rho_crit/rho_P = (3/8pi)(l_P/r_H)^2 explains the notorious
   "10^122 cosmological constant problem" as a degree-of-freedom counting
   error (volume vs. surface).

2. SIX-DATASET VERIFICATION AT UNPRECEDENTED SCALE. We jointly fit six
   independent cosmological datasets spanning six orders of magnitude in
   physical scale:
     - 1,701 Pantheon+SH0ES Type Ia supernovae (full covariance)
     - 1,820 DES-SN5YR distance moduli (full covariance)
     - 12 DESI DR2 BAO measurements
     - 196 SPT-3G TT/TE/EE bandpowers (via candl)
     - 122 ACT DR6 TT/TE/EE bandpowers (via candl)
     - 175 SPARC galaxy rotation curves (per-galaxy M/L fits)
   
   The joint MLE gives chi^2/dof = 0.9662 across 3,851 data points ---
   an extraordinary fit for a minimal-parameter model. The production
   MCMC (64 walkers x 600 steps, 50.67 GPU-hours) gives:
     Lambda = (1.117 +/- 0.022) x 10^-52 m^-2
     H0 = 68.09 +/- 0.50 km/s/Mpc
     Omm = 0.3101 +/- 0.0048

3. THEORETICAL STAKE ON THE HUBBLE TENSION. Our holographic prior places
   H0 = 68.09 +/- 0.50 km/s/Mpc, consistent with Planck at 0.7 sigma and
   4.6 sigma below SH0ES. This provides independent theoretical motivation
   for the growing body of evidence (JWST Cepheids, TRGB, JAGB methods)
   suggesting the high-H0 SH0ES value requires calibration revision.
   The alternative holographic dark energy model (Li 2004) is decisively
   rejected by our data (DELTA_BIC = +57.9).

SIGNIFICANCE FOR NATURE ASTRONOMY

The cosmological constant problem (why is Lambda so small?) and the
Hubble tension (why do early and late measurements disagree?) are two of
the most prominent open problems in astronomy. This paper provides
(a) a coherent theoretical framework that explains the magnitude of Lambda
from first principles, and (b) a comprehensive multi-probe verification
that yields H0 consistent with CMB-based values. The ten falsifiable
predictions (DESI Year-5, Euclid, Simons Observatory, Einstein Telescope)
make this work immediately testable.

SUITABILITY AND ORIGINALITY

cosmological fit by a single open-source pipeline.
